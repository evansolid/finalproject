Options for Action with Ethical Analysis


There are many different ways for artificial intelligence to develop in the future. Here are three potential solutions to the concerns raised in this website.


Option 1: No AI Services


Enforce a no-AI rule on healthcare services, preventing them from using any tools that may lead to the privacy violation of any user. In addition, discourage use elsewhere, in an effort to help reduce environmental damage. This option respects the privacy of patients over everything, including potentially better care. This may help to prevent other kinds of harm from bad actors who would use their personal information in a negative way or environmental harm from the AI. This does the most good for a larger community and group of people at the expense of individuals who will receive worse care. This most closely lines up with Virtue Ethics, as the healthcare services will be acting to not use the untrustworthy and potentially unethical software regardless of outcome.


Option 2: AI Use with Oversight


Allow AI use, but only after developing detailed privacy preservation procedures and ensuring that all data will be handled properly. This would provide healthcare services with better ways to diagnose and treat patients while preserving their privacy and likely be self-hosted servers but more expensive. This option respects privacy of everyone as well as aiming to provide the best care for all patients, at the expense of potential higher cost for electricity and hardware and environmental damage resulting. This option most closely aligns with a Communitarian Lens and more than the other two with the Care Ethics Lens as it aims to provide the best care and privacy for all members of a community, regardless of the costs. This option balances the privacy of the patients and the health of the environment with the patients’ right to good healthcare, as well as the duties of the healthcare workers and developers (to make/use effective but also safe technology). While some individuals may be harmed by the environmental impacts or by the potential loss of accuracy or effectiveness of technology that gets sacrificed in favor of privacy, the community as a whole benefits from this option, so the communitarian lens would support this option.


Option 3: Full AI Use Allowed


Allow AI to use larger, deprivatized data sets, at the cost of major changes to HIPAA privacy rules and potential security vulnerabilities by trusting the outsourced technology. This option may provide better care options to healthcare workers and work for software developers, but runs the risk of imposing on privacy laws and making personal data more susceptible to leaks and hacking. Notably, this option does not respect individual privacy in the aim to use the technology to its fullest to provide even better and more personalized care. This leaves open options for bad actors to take information from non-secure AI servers and cause damage with it. The electricity and environmental damages will also be high. This does not align with any ethical lens more than the others as it is an action that will likely lead to more environmental and societal harm than good, however it provides an example of a worse decision that is applicable to the real world as it is similar to real-world actions being taken by some healthcare service groups.
