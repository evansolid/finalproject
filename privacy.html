How is Your Privacy at Risk and What Happens if it is Released?


Data breaches in healthcare have been getting more and more frequent. Between October 2009 and January 2026, there have been over 7000 large healthcare data breaches, and these are just the ones that were reported. One of the biggest healthcare breaches to date occurred in 2024, when Change Healthcare (part of the UnitedHealth Group) was subject to a healthcare breach. Change handled approximately 1 in 3 patient records, which range from insurance claims to prescriptions and so on. A Russian ransomware group encrypted and incapacitated much of Change’s data, which had massive impacts on healthcare providers and patients across the country. Many were subject to delayed authorization of necessary care, months-long delays of day-to-day operations, and revenue loss and other financial impacts.

Data from OCR suggests that data breaches are moving away from theft and towards hacking or IT incidents, and that in recent years data breaches have started affecting more people on average than 20 years ago. These violations can also cost healthcare providers millions of dollars penalty fees for violating HIPAA.
