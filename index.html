AI and Data Privacy in Healthcare


Most people trust that their sensitive medical information will be protected when they seek care, but some healthcare providers are using new AI technology to assist their workers, which could put this information at risk of being leaked. From scanning for cancerous tumors to having AI chatbots that can classify patient symptoms and recommend action, there are many ways that AI and machine learning are becoming integrated into the healthcare system. This change has many benefits, such as assisting diagnoses of life-threatening illnesses and providing custom treatment. However, this comes at the cost of several drawbacks, such as causing massive ecological damage and the major concern that its use would require accessing private and secure patient data, which may open it up to cyberattacks and possibly having it be leaked to other parties. We will look at all sides of this issue to come to an understanding of what this technology is and the concerns that exist around it.



There are many benefits to using AI in healthcare settings, from custom treatment strategies to better predictive analytics. However, in order to get access to this level of treatment, the AI needs to have access to EHR, or Electronic Health Records. This is currently a HIPAA violation, as healthcare services have very strict patient information privacy rules. However, recent changes have meant that some services are being allowed to use this data to provide better treatment to patients by using information such as their age, gender, ethnicity, address, insurance status, and more. Unfortunately, AI agents and the data they use is not protected by strong privacy protections and opens up opportunities for cyber criminals to steal the information, potentially leaking sensitive data of many individuals. This can affect many people, from the individuals whose information is leaked to relatives, healthcare professionals, and even the software developers making the AI tools. There are many ways, however, to combat this issue and work is ongoing to keep the information safe while allowing them to use it for healthcare. A final consideration is the environmental impact of using AI, which can cause massive ecological damage from the removal of land to mine for heavy metals used in the chips or draining of water to use in cooling in the data centers.
